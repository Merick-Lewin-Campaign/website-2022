About Merick

Merick Lewin has a proven record as a successful small business owner and community and non-profit leader. As the Chairperson of Take Stock in Children of Broward County, Merick has worked to improve the education outcomes of our students and was recognized for his efforts by 211 Broward as the 2022 Non-Profit Board Leader of the Year. Married to an amazing Broward County High School Counselor, Michelle Lewin, education is a shared passion in the Lewin Household. Merick and Michelle have a one year old son, whose future public education along with the experiences above drove Merick to seek a position on the school board and dedicate himself to work every day to improve our schools.

Campaign Focus

Clear and consistent communication between me and the families, students, teachers, staff, and all stakeholders in the district. My door will always be open, my phone will always be on. I will be as vigilant about your child's education as you are. When the School Board has successes, you will know. When we fall short, you will know. When we face challenges, you will know. When we need help, you will know. When I am your School Board member, KNOW you will always be informed.

I will make sure all students in our schools are given the opportunity to reach their highest potential. This will be achieved through increasing access to mental health resources as well as the promotion of all the clubs, teams, programs, and extracurricular activities that give children a sense of belonging and a chance at excellence. Schools must be places that foster the unique skills, talents, and interests of our students, letting each one discover what makes them special.

All students should have access to the post-secondary outcome that fits them best and gives them the greatest chance at success and fulfillment. Students who want to go to college should graduate from our schools with the skills and tools that allow them to succeed. Students who want to explore trade, technical, military, and workforce post-secondary options should be given the same level of information, resources, and support as our college bound students. ALL CHILDREN DESERVE to follow their dreams.
